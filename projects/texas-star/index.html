Background

The Texas Star has been a Dallas icon since 1985 after being purchased from a company in Italy for 2.2 million dollars, or 5.2 million dollars in today's money.

Location of Texas Star in Fair Park

Read More on Wikipedia Official Fair Park Website

Stats

Texas Star is twenty stories tall (216 feet) and is 203 feet in diameter, making it the largest ferris wheel in North America.
 
The wheel has 45 gondolas carrying 270 people, it makes 1.5 revolutions every minute. A full ride lasts 12 minutes.
 
Texas Star is the most popular ride at the State Fair of Texas.

Synopsis

Staffed by a crew of 15, the Texas Star loads each of it's 45 gondolas from the bottom, or 0 feet. The ferris wheel makes a rotation every 1.5 minutes, 90 seconds. Any given gondola reaches it's maximum height of 101.5 feet every 45 seconds. At this point, it's possible to see the city of Fort Worth's skyline on a clear day.

A gondola returns to 0 feet at 90 seconds and continues the cycle for the duration of the ride: 12 minutes or 720 seconds.
