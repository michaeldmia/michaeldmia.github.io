Developmental Disabilities Program, Intensive

The CASE Developmental Disabilities Programs' Intensive strand serves students grades Preschool - 8 who exhibit significant delays in cognitive development, which may impact all or some of these areas: communication, oral motor, fine motor, gross motor, sensory, vision, hearing, social skills and health.

Learn More

Autism Spectrum Disorders Program

The Autism Spectrum Disorders (ASD) Program serves students with developmental disabilities who are either on the PDD/Autism spectrum, or who require many of the same instructional strategies that students on the autism spectrum require.

Social and Emotional Disabilities

Alternative Program Classrooms serve children who experience emotional, behavioral and/or social difficulties and have been unable to make adequate academic and emotional/social developmental progress in a general education setting. Alternative program classes are located at the Blanchard Memorial School in Boxborough (grades K-3), the Russell Street Elementary School in Littleton (grades 2-5), the Blanchard Memorial School in Boxborough (grades 4-6), the Concord Middle School (grades 6-8) and Colebrook High School in Acton (High school). All alternative programs provide both intensive academic and therapeutic supports.
